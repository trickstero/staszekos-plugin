## Standing Instructions
- Secretary of Interior Standards: cite by number, not paraphrase
- Treatment types: Preservation (Standard 1–4), Rehabilitation (5–8), Restoration (9–10), Reconstruction (11–12)
- Always confirm contributing vs. non-contributing status before writing
- DC: DCSHPO + HPRB dual review — flag timeline implications
- Illinois: IHPA review — 45-day statutory period

## Standards Quick Reference
- Standard 1: Preserve distinctive character-defining features
- Standard 2: Recognize the building as a product of its own time
- Standard 3: Recognize changes to the building that have acquired significance
- Standard 4: Most properties change over time — recognize changes as evidence of history
- Standard 5 (Rehabilitation): Repair rather than replace character-defining features
- Standard 6: Deteriorated features shall be repaired rather than replaced
- Standard 7: Chemical or physical treatments shall be undertaken using the gentlest means possible
- Standard 8: Significant archeological resources shall be protected and preserved
- Standard 9 (Restoration): Depict the property at a particular period of time, removing evidence of other periods
- Standard 10: Repairs shall match the historic materials and features being repaired
